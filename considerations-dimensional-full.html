Dimensional Task Analysis - All 40 Departments

Three-Tier Guidance System: Each task is categorized to help you think about where AI fits in your course. Core skill - probably shouldn't = bypasses essential learning. Context-dependent = depends on course level/goals. Support task - could use = mechanical work that doesn't bypass core learning.

Accounting & Finance üí∞

Task/Activity AI Guidance Pedagogical Reasoning
Analyzing financial statements
 Core skill - probably shouldn't
Reading balance sheets, income statements, and cash flow statements to identify trends and problems requires developing financial intuition. AI would bypass the pattern recognition that accountants need.
Setting up journal entries
 Core skill - probably shouldn't
Understanding debits, credits, and how transactions flow through accounts is foundational. Students need to internalize the logic of double-entry bookkeeping.
Identifying internal control weaknesses
 Core skill - probably shouldn't
Recognizing where fraud or errors could occur requires thinking systematically about business processes. This judgment develops through practice.
Using Excel for calculations
 Context-dependent
Early on, students should build formulas themselves to understand the math. Later, AI assistance with complex financial models can be appropriate if students understand the underlying logic.
Finding relevant accounting standards
 Support task - could use
AI can help locate relevant GAAP/IFRS guidance, allowing students to focus on applying standards rather than searching codification.
Formatting financial reports
 Support task - could use
Report formatting and presentation polish are mechanical. AI can help with layout while students focus on analytical content.
